About me

I have been a full-time Web developer since 2008, working primarily in PHP, HTML5, CSS/LESS, and JavaScript (including jQuery). Additionally, I have worked with MySQL and Oracle databases, and I am right at home on the Unix command line. I try to embrace the best qualities of each language to write maintainable, testable, object-oriented code.

Since April, 2013, I have been a senior web developer at BAE Systems Applied Intelligence (formerly SilverSky) in Boston, MA. My work has focused on BAE-AI´s web portal, used by customers to manage their various security services. I have led a push toward leaner, modular, and unit-testable code; the use of dependency-management tools like Composer and Bower; and developer communication and feedback through code reviews. Often I am called upon by other developers as a mentor, and as an authority on best practices, a role I thoroughly enjoy and am actively seeking to do more of.

From April, 2008 until March, 2013, I was a Web developer at Blue State Digital´s Boston, MA, office. Known for supplying the technology that helped lead to the November, 2008, presidential election of Barack Obama, BSD provided an arena for enthusiastic Web developers to sharpen their skills. I helped develop and maintain the company´s Web toolset, working on features such as forms for honorary or in memoriam donations and emailable greeting cards. During the Obama campaign, I was one of two developers who helped support BSD´s numerous other political and non-profit clients, often helping to build out sites using Expression Engine. Later during my tenure, I led development on a team that introduced chaptering to the company´s offerings, allowing large organizations to manage their overall toolsets while giving their local chapters varying levels of autonomy in managing their own sites.
